This project and data was used to complete a final project for EPI 522 Analytic Methods for Epidemiology. A course taken for the completion of Harvard University MPH-45 Epidemiology degree. 
This course covered a wide variety of methods used to analyze epidemiologic data. 
It started with a review of the basic principles of causal inference and the use of causal diagrams to identify confounding. 
Review regression-based methods to control for confounding, including logistic regression and propensity score analysis. 
The course also covered survival analysis and Cox proportional hazards regression for time-to-event data, extensions of logistic regression (including ordinal logistic regression, multinomial logistic regression, and conditional logistic regression),and methodsfor developing and validating prediction rules based on regression models.
Finally, the course discussed methods for handling missing data.
